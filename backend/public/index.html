The Burning Bush

The golden dawn light cuts through the dust of the wadi. Your sheep bleat nervously. Before you, the bush burns fiercely, tongues of flame wrapping the branches without consuming them. Heat radiates against your skin, carrying the scent of scorched earth and something sacred. The voice rolls over you like thunder wrapped in mercy, echoing covenants made with flawed men before you. "Eliab, son of Jesse. The kingdoms fracture. Blood will stain the throne before the anointed rises. Choose how you will walk this path of fire and flesh."
